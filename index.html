Other maritime roles and how you match them:
Design Engineer / Operations Specialist (DE)
You enjoy marrying technology, creativity and logic to fix real-world problems. In the maritime world, Design Engineers and Operations Specialists help design and run smarter, greener and more efficient ships and port systems – from exploring cleaner fuels to digital tools that make ships and ports safer and more sustainable.
Ship Broker (SB)
You’re a natural people person who enjoys convincing others and making win–win deals. Ship Brokers help match ships with cargo around the world, working across cultures and time zones. They blend sharp commercial instincts with strong communication skills to keep global trade moving.
Seafarer (SF)
Independent and adventurous, you’re resilient at sea and enjoy seeing the world from port to port. You value quiet off-grid time and strong camaraderie with your crew as you handle cargo operations, maintenance and navigation together.
Vessel Traffic Officer (VTO)
You stay calm under pressure and think clearly when things get messy. Vessel Traffic Officers monitor ship movements, maintain navigational safety and respond quickly when unexpected situations occur – giving clear instructions and advice to keep everyone safe.
Port Inspector (PI)
You’re protective by nature and pay attention to people’s well-being. Port Inspectors act as first responders for incidents at sea or in port, checking safety standards and coordinating with agencies such as the police and coast guard to help people in distress and enforce rules.
Hydrographic Surveyor (HS)
You’re analytical and like making sense of data. Hydrographic Surveyors measure sea depths and the shape of the seabed to help update nautical charts. They’re the ones who can “see” what’s going on under the water’s surface, helping ships navigate safely and supporting coastal development and environmental projects.
